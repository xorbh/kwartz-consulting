# Kwartz Consulting

## Blog tone

- Be gentle. Let data and examples speak for themselves — don't accuse or imply the reader is doing things wrong.
- Avoid "most organizations are broken" framing. Present insights as opportunities, not indictments.
- Don't use "this isn't X, it's Y" reveal patterns — they read as AI-generated.
- Write conversationally, not performatively. Direct sentences, no dramatic buildup.
- The audience is leaders driving change. Respect that they're already trying. Meet them where they are.
- Avoid em-dashes (—). Use periods, commas, or restructure the sentence instead.
